Music Projects

if...else (Live at Oliver Colvin Hall)

Date: Recorded Live on 10/10/2025

Role: Composer, Bandleader, Pianist

if...else is an 8-track album of original jazz fusion music, recorded live in concert at Berklee's Oliver Colvin Hall. The album's title reflects its musical concept: blending traditional jazz forms with the conditional "twists" of modern subgenres, from funk and DnB to swing and ballad.

As the composer and bandleader for the 5-piece ensemble, I was responsible for all original compositions and arrangements. I also managed all rehearsals and directed the final live performance and recording session from the piano.

Evigilatio Aeternitatis

Date: August 2025

Role: Composer, Producer, Sound Designer

Titled from the Latin for “Awakening of Eternity,” Evigilatio Aeternitatis is a 6-minute, 28-second electronic composition that explores ideas of vigilance, memory, and time stretching outward. The piece weaves granular textures, layered harmonies, and evolving rhythmic figures into an immersive soundscape that gradually shifts rather than moving through a traditional structure.

As the composer, producer, and sound designer, I created every aspect of the work from timbre to harmony to structure, using a combination of synthesis, sampling, and digital audio processing in Ableton Live. The piece reflects my interest in storytelling through sound alone: using gradual transformations of texture and space to suggest narrative and emotional change.

Beyond Horizons

Date: May 2025

Role: Composer, Producer, Sound Designer

Beyond Horizons is a 4-minute, 45-second electronic composition created entirely with modular synthesis, using the Buchla 200e, the Make Noise Shared System, and additional effect processing. The piece explores themes of exploration and the unknown, using evolving timbres, shifting textures, and dynamic contrasts to evoke a sense of travelling into uncharted sonic territory.

As the sole artist, I handled all aspects of composition, sound design, and production, working through hands-on patching and real-time control. The piece reflects my interest in treating modular instruments as exploratory systems, where gesture, circuitry, and sound continually influence one another to shape the listening experience.

Crank Up - EP

Date: March 2025

Role: Composer, Producer, Designer

Crank Up is a 3-song avant-garde electronic EP that explores the interplay between rigid mathematical structure and diverse sonic materials. A unifying thread across the collection is the Fibonacci ratio, which dictates the three-part form of each piece.

The EP serves as a technical and artistic trilogy, with each track focusing on a different synthesis and compositional method:

  • Anima Ex Machina: A musique-concrète piece using tape techniques on recorded samples to satirize industry conformity.
  • Cold Junction: A composition built entirely from three noise oscillators in VCV Rack.
  • Crank Up: An homage to John Chowning, using two-operator FM synthesis to create a complex, evolving composition.

As the sole artist, I was responsible for all composition, production, and sound design, as well as designing the original cover art.

Stratocumulus

Date: November 2025

Role: Independent Developer (Max, gen~, JavaScript, C++, JUCE)

Repo: View on GitHub

Design Document: Download PDF

Stratocumulus is a real-time audio plugin that transforms monophonic audio into a polyphonic granular instrument. Prototyped in Max/MSP, it is deployed both as a Max for Live device with a custom JavaScript UI and as a C++ JUCE plugin.

The system combines a YIN-inspired fundamental frequency tracker with a MIDI-driven granular synthesis engine, enabling harmonically consistent, performance-ready control of live vocal or instrumental signals.

I designed and implemented the full system architecture, DSP, and user interface from scratch, and documented the project in a LaTeX design paper modeled after an academic article.

Mutator

Date: August 2025

Role: Independent Developer (Max, gen~)

Repo: View on GitHub

Mutator is a multi-sequencing effector Max for Live device developed in Max/MSP. It features 6 audio effects in sequential order - pitch shifter, beat repeat, flanger, phaser, downsampler, and Schroeder reverb, all developed in the gen~ environment.

The device's core is its sequencer, which triggers each effect's on/off state. It automatically syncs its step count to Ableton's time signature. You can set the step length (1/4, 1/8, 1/16) with dotted or triplet rhythms over a 1 or 2-bar cycle. Each effect has one main parameter (scaled 0-100) for easy control, plus a global dry/wet mix. The UI also features a dynamic color palette that selects from 12 presets on load.

As the developer, I built all components from scratch except for the downsampler, which is from a built-in tutorial from Cycling '74.

PythonicSynth

Date: May 2024

Role: Independent Developer (Python)

Repo: View on GitHub

PythonicSynth is an additive synthesizer built entirely in Python. This project was an exploration of building a complete audio application from scratch outside of a typical plugin environment.

The audio engine, developed using the Pyo library, features six oscillators with individual controls and multiple effectors. I scripted a universal ADSR envelope with exponential control to shape the synth's amplitude. The entire graphical user interface was also custom-built using the wxPython library.
